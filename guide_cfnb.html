Mentality & Approach
This event is not won in the first six hours. It is survived in the last six.

Pace yourself from WOD one. Your job is not to post a hero time, your job is to still be standing when your next slot comes around at 0200.

Break it into blocks. Morning, afternoon, evening, overnight. One block at a time. The moment you start doing the maths on how many hours are left you have already lost the mental game.

Expect a wall somewhere between midnight and 0300. It is not a sign that something is wrong. It is a sign that you are a human being doing something genuinely questionable.

When you hit it, and you will, remember why this event exists. Every WOD carries the name of a real person. A soldier, a police officer, a firefighter who did not get to choose when things got hard. We do. That is the privilege and that is the point. Eat something, dig in, and keep moving.

Also remind yourself that Rinus signed up for 209 vested burpees. So whatever you are feeling right now, your problems are not that serious.

The names on those WODs did not tap out.
Neither do we.

Team Classified · April 18-19, 2026
Workout Strategy
Start every WOD at 80 percent. The other 20 is there if you need it later.
General rule

Break sets before you have to, not when you are forced to. Planned rest is faster than failing a rep and staring at the bar.

Running WODs

The run is your recovery between barbell and gymnastics work. Use it. Do not sprint out of the gate and crawl back in.

High rep bodyweight (Aplin, Akker)

Pace brutally from the first set. Failing at rep 200 because you went too hard at rep one is a very specific kind of suffering that is entirely avoidable.

Overnight WODs

Your coordination drops more than you think after midnight. Go lighter than your ego wants and move well. The barbell does not care that it is 0100.

Partner and team WODs

Communicate constantly. Use the built-in rest periods. A well managed partner WOD will leave you fresher than a solo WOD where you redlined.

Heavy barbell WODs (Walshy 142, Palmer)

Warm up more than you think you need to. Cold muscles and heavy barbells at night are not friends. Scale the load early if movement quality drops.

Recovery, Stretching & Mobility
Do not sit down and get cold after a WOD. The version of you at 0400 will feel the difference.

First 10-15 minutes after every WOD

Walk it off. Easy bike or row. Get the heart rate down gradually. Get your breathing under control before you eat or drink anything substantial.

Short gaps (1-2 hours)

Light mobility on whatever you just loaded. Do not stretch cold, move first. Keep a layer on. A cold muscle between WODs is asking for a strain.

Longer gaps (4+ hours)

Twenty minutes of deliberate mobility work pays dividends six hours later. Elevate your legs. A cool shower on the legs reduces localised inflammation. Not comfortable, very effective.

Key areas to hit across the event

Hips and hip flexors

Couch stretch, pigeon, 90/90. After any squat or running heavy WOD.

Thoracic spine

Foam roller or lacrosse ball on the upper back. Keeps pressing and overhead positions open. Two minutes consistently beats one long session you never do.

Ankles and calves

Roll the calves out, ankle circles. Three minutes. Prevents the wooden leg feeling at 0400.

Shoulders and lats

Doorframe stretches, lat hangs, cross body stretch. After any pull or press heavy WOD.

Hamstrings and lower back

Forward fold, supine hamstring stretch, child's pose. Do not neglect these overnight or getting out of the car the next morning will be an experience.

Nutrition
Your body cannot run on willpower alone. Unfortunately.

Pre WOD

Something small and carb focused 60 to 90 minutes out. Banana, rice cakes with honey, oats, toast with peanut butter. Not a full meal. Avoid high fat and high fibre -- they slow digestion and make the WOD uncomfortable.

Overnight WODs

You will not feel like eating. Eat anyway. A small carb snack 45 minutes out is better than going in empty.

Caffeine

Save your biggest hit for the 0100 to 0300 window. Using it all day means it stops working when things get hard. Small coffee 30 to 45 minutes pre WOD is fine. Do not live on it.

Intra WOD

Water for anything under 20 minutes. Electrolyte drink for anything near the 40 minute cap. Sip it, do not skull it. Add electrolytes to at least every second drink across the whole day -- plain water alone will not replace what you lose in sweat over 24 hours.

Post WOD

Protein and carbs within 30 minutes. A shake and a banana works. 20 to 30g protein, similar amount of carbs. Overnight, liquid nutrition is your friend. A protein drink is easier to get down at 0300 than a chicken breast, and considerably less depressing.

General strategy

Real food during the day while digestion works well. Simpler, more digestible food from 2000 onwards. Keep snacks accessible at all times. Something in the hand between WODs is better than a full meal you have to think about.

Do not wait until you are thirsty. By the time you are thirsty you are already behind.

Napping & Sleep
A nap is not a luxury. It is a performance tool. Plan it like one.

After 18 hours awake your performance drops significantly. After 24 hours you are effectively operating at the same level as someone who is legally drunk. This is not a metaphor. Plan your sleep accordingly.

10-20 min

Power nap. Best for short gaps. Refreshes without grogginess. Set two alarms. Always two.

90 min

Full sleep cycle. Use for gaps of 3 hours or more. Wake up feeling like a different person.

Avoid 30 to 60 minute naps. You will wake up mid cycle, groggy, confused, and furious at everyone. This is not what the team needs at 0200.

General napping rules

Before you sleep

Identify your rest gaps in advance and plan which nap length fits. Do not leave it to chance at hour sixteen.

Alarms

Set two alarms. Always two. Tell someone when you are sleeping so they can wake you if needed.

Environment

Sleep somewhere quiet and dark if possible. Pack an eye mask and earplugs -- they make a real difference in a busy gym environment.

Timing

Do not nap within 60 minutes of your next WOD. The grogginess will not clear in time and you will perform worse than if you had stayed awake.

Waking up

Splash cold water on your face immediately. Get up and walk around. Eat something small. Give yourself 10 minutes before you assess how you feel.

Longer gaps (6+ hours)

A 90 minute sleep cycle is your target. Use the time well -- eat first, then sleep, then mobility before your next WOD. Do not waste a long gap lying awake on your phone.

Body Awareness & Managing Load
Discomfort is expected. You signed up for discomfort. Damage is different.

Sharp, localised, sudden pain that does not ease off with movement is not discomfort. If you feel that, stop and tell someone. Pushing through actual damage in hour eight means you may not make it to hour sixteen.

Watch for

Joint pain that worsens across WODs rather than improving with warm up. Grip and forearm failure on pull work -- if your grip is gone, scale the movement. Lower back tightening under deadlift and squat volume -- manage it with mobility, and reduce load if it goes from tight to painful.

Dizziness or nausea

Almost always nutrition or hydration. Sit down, eat something, drink something. Wait five minutes before continuing. Do not try to walk it off mid WOD before you become a team problem.

Somewhere around 0200 most people will have a moment where they question every life decision that led them here. When that moment comes, look at the name on the whiteboard. That person did not get to tap out. Neither do we. Eat something, talk to a teammate, and finish what you started.
